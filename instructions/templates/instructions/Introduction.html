Decision rounds

Today's experiment consists of 25 decision rounds. Each round is separate, and your choices in one round do not affect the results of any other round.

Your options

In each round, you will choose one out of two options.

Your earnings in a round depend on the option you choose, and a random draw. You can think of this as a draw from a virtual bag containing balls numbered 1 to 100, where each ball is equally likely to be drawn.

Each option is represented by a bar, which shows how the earnings from that option depend on the draw. Here is a typical option:

The top of the bar gives the number of balls which correspond to each potential earnings amount. The bottom shows the ranges of corresponding ball numbers.

For this option,

  • 35 balls (numbered 1 to 35) would result in earnings of £0;
  • 25 balls (numbered 36 to 60) would result in earnings of £10;
  • 40 balls (numbered 61 to 100) would result in earnings of £20.
