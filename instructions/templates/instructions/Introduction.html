Decision rounds

Today's experiment consists of 25 decision rounds. Each round is separate, and your choices in one round do not affect the results of any other round.

Your options

In each round, you will make a choice of one out of two options. A typical option will look like the sample one below:

If you were to choose this option in a round, the amount you would earn would depend on the outcome of a random draw. There is a virtual bag containing one hundred (100) balls, numbered 1 through 100 inclusive. The random draw consists of picking one ball at random from this bag. Each ball is therefore equally likely to be chosen.

The horizontal bar tells you how your earnings depend on which ball is drawn. Across the bottom of the bar are numbers which label the ranges of ball numbers which correspond to each potential earnings amount:

  • If the ball drawn has a number between 61 and 100, you would earn £20;
  • If the ball drawn has a number between 36 and 60, you would earn £10;
  • If the ball drawn has a number between 1 and 35, you would earn £0.
