Decision rounds

Today's experiment consists of 25 decision rounds. Each round is separate, and your choices in one round do not affect the results of any other round.

Your options

In each round, you will make a choice of one out of two options. The amount you will earn from choosing an option will depend on the outcome of a random draw.

There is a virtual bag containing one hundred (100) balls, numbered 1 through 100 inclusive. The random draw consists of picking one ball at random from this bag. Each ball is equally likely to be chosen.

The way in which your earnings depend on the draw of the ball will differ for each option. Here is a sample option:

This horizontal bar tells you how your earnings from the option will depend on which ball is drawn. Across the bottom of the bar are numbers which label the ranges of ball numbers which correspond to each potential earnings amount:

  • If the ball drawn has a number between 61 and 100, you would earn £20;
  • If the ball drawn has a number between 36 and 60, you would earn £10;
  • If the ball drawn has a number between 1 and 35, you would earn £0.
