{% if session.config.show_expected and session.config.show_risk %}

To the right of each option are two numbers.

The first number is labelled Average returns. You can think of this as the amount you would earn if we were to do a large number of random draws, and compute the average earnings per draw. Remember however that your earnings from the option will be determined by a single draw only.

The second number is labelled Variability score. This is the standard deviation, which is a measure of how spread out the earnings are. The earnings from an option with a higher standard deviation are more variable than the earnings from an option with a lower standard deviation.

You can review these definitions during the experiment by clicking on or hovering over the Average returns and Variability score labels.
